Units in CSS

In CSS, there are two main types of units: absolute units and relative units. Absolute units are fixed and do not change based on the context, while relative units are flexible and adjust based on the context, such as the size of the parent element. Absolute units include cm (centimeters), mm (millimeters), in (inches), and px (pixels). Relative units include em (relative to the font-size of the parent element), rem (relative to the font-size of the root element), % (relative to the size of the parent element), vw (relative to 1% of the viewport's width), and vh (relative to 1% of the viewport's height).
